Digital technology is shaping the world. But can it also help sustain it?

This book explores how digital technologies influence the environment, society and the economy, and how IT professionals can use that knowledge to make more responsible choices. Combining academic insight with practical examples, it introduces sustainability as a central concern in digital work.

The result is a clear foundation for working with technology more reflectively, responsibly and with an eye to the future. For anyone studying or working with IT, this book is an invitation to help build a digital future that is not just smarter, but greener, healthier and more durable.

Read this book, and discover how information technology and digitalisation can help build a greener, healthier, and brighter digital future.

András Ács Pedersen is an associate professor in IT and digital communication at Zealand, where he teaches and conducts research in IT security, user experience, digital value creation, and sustainable IT. He is the author of several textbooks and a frequent speaker on digitalisation, cybersecurity, and sustainability.

Jesper Balslev, PhD, is an associate professor at Business Academy Copenhagen. His research examines deceptive design and the barriers, dilemmas, and paradoxes businesses face when working with sustainability requirements. He has authored and co-authored several textbooks and academic publications.
